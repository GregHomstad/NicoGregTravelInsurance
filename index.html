Common questions

What if my parents have pre-existing medical conditions?

All plans include coverage for pre-existing condition emergencies, from $500 (Ultra Plus) to $6,000 (VIP Plus). This covers unexpected flare-ups and emergencies related to existing conditions. If your parents have chronic conditions like diabetes or heart disease, we recommend VIP or VIP Plus for higher limits.

My parents are over 70. Can they still get coverage?

Yes — we cover travelers up to age 84 on daily plans (5–90 days). Most competitors cap at 70 or 75. For extended stays over 60 days, the age limit is 65.

Can I buy this for my parents from the US?

Absolutely. Most of our customers do exactly this. You fill in the trip details and pay from the US. Your parents receive the policy documents by email, and you can forward the traveler guide to them.

How does "direct hospital payment" work?

When your parents have a medical emergency, they (or you) contact the assistance center. The plan coordinates with the hospital and the covered amount is paid directly — your parents don't need to put it on a credit card and wait for reimbursement. Pre-authorization is required for non-emergency/planned care.

What happens if something goes wrong after hours or on a weekend?

The BMI Travel Assist center handles emergencies around the clock. Your parents can reach it using the contact details on their voucher.

Is this actual insurance?

TripKavach plans are travel assistance products provided by BMI Travel Assist, a subsidiary of BMI Financial Group. They are not a substitute for health insurance, Medicare, or social security programs. They cover emergencies and unexpected events during international travel.

Can I cancel if plans change?

Yes. If you cancel before the coverage start date (the traveler's departure date), you can receive a full refund. Once coverage has started, cancellations are not available.

What about pre-existing conditions?

All plans include coverage for emergencies related to pre-existing conditions, ranging from $500 (Ultra Plus) to $6,000 (VIP Plus). This covers unexpected flare-ups, not routine care or follow-ups.

What's NOT covered?

Routine check-ups and wellness visits, elective or cosmetic procedures, pre-planned surgeries, substance abuse treatment, and injuries from extreme sports (unless covered by the amateur sports benefit). Always review the full conditions of coverage before purchasing.

Can I cancel if my trip plans change?

Yes. If you cancel before your departure date, you can receive a full refund. Once your coverage period has started, cancellations are not available.

What if I need help at 2am?

The BMI Travel Assist center handles emergencies around the clock. Contact details are on your voucher.

How quickly are claims paid?

For direct hospital payments, we coordinate in real-time during your emergency. For reimbursement claims, review takes approximately 5 business days after we receive complete documentation, with payment issued within 30 days of approval.

Conditions of Coverage

Last updated: March 2026

This page summarizes key conditions. For the full legal terms, refer to your policy voucher and the BMI Travel Assist General Conditions document.

1. Eligibility

BMI Travel Assist plans are available to travelers who meet the following criteria:

  • Age limits: Ages 0–84 for daily (short-term) plans; ages 0–65 for long-stay plans.
  • Residency: The insured must depart from and return to their home country of residence. Plans are not available to residents of the United States.
  • Travel purpose: Coverage is not available if the primary purpose of travel is to receive medical treatment abroad for a pre-existing condition.
  • Legal status: No coverage is provided for beneficiaries in illegal immigration or work status.

2. Coverage Period

Your coverage begins on the departure date specified in your policy voucher and ends on the return date.

  • Daily (short-term) plans: Maximum duration of 90 days per trip.
  • Long-stay plans: Maximum duration of 365 days per trip.
  • Coverage is valid only during the dates shown on your voucher. Any events occurring before or after these dates are not covered.

3. Pre-existing Conditions

Pre-existing medical conditions receive limited coverage under BMI Travel Assist plans:

  • What is covered: Acute emergencies and unexpected flare-ups of pre-existing conditions only. This means sudden, unforeseen episodes that require immediate medical attention.
  • What is NOT covered: Routine care, scheduled follow-ups, planned treatments, or any medical services related to the ongoing management of a pre-existing condition.
  • Coverage limits: Pre-existing condition coverage amounts vary by plan tier, ranging from $500 to $6,000 depending on the plan selected.
  • Stability requirement: The pre-existing condition must have been stable for at least 60 days prior to the departure date. A condition is considered stable if there has been no change in treatment, medication, or symptoms during that period.

4. What's Covered

BMI Travel Assist plans provide coverage for the following benefits (subject to plan tier and coverage limits):

  • Medical emergencies: Treatment for accidents and sudden illnesses, including hospitalization, surgery, specialist consultations, and prescribed medications.
  • Emergency dental: Treatment for sudden dental pain or trauma to natural teeth.
  • Medical evacuation and repatriation: Emergency medical transport to the nearest adequate facility, and repatriation to the home country when medically necessary.
  • Trip cancellation: Reimbursement for non-refundable trip costs if your trip is cancelled due to a covered reason (available on select plans).
  • Lost baggage: Compensation for checked baggage that is lost, stolen, or damaged by a common carrier.
  • Flight delays: Coverage for expenses incurred due to a significant flight delay (meals, accommodation, essential items).
  • Accidental death and dismemberment: A lump-sum benefit payable in the event of accidental death or qualifying permanent injury.

5. What's NOT Covered

The following are expressly excluded from coverage under all plans:

  • Routine check-ups, wellness visits, and preventive screenings.
  • Elective or cosmetic procedures, including but not limited to plastic surgery, laser eye surgery, and dental implants.
  • Pre-planned surgeries or medical procedures that were scheduled before the coverage period began.
  • Conditions arising from substance abuse, including drug and alcohol use.
  • Injuries sustained during extreme or professional sports, unless the amateur sports benefit applies to your plan. Excluded activities include motorsports, diving below 15 meters, hang gliding, mountaineering, bungee jumping, and other high-risk sports.
  • Pregnancy-related conditions after 26 weeks of gestation (unless the Maternity upgrade is purchased).
  • Self-inflicted injuries, suicide, or attempted suicide.
  • Injuries or losses resulting from war, armed conflict, terrorism, or civil unrest.
  • Nuclear events, radiation exposure, or radioactive contamination.
  • Mental health and psychological conditions, including panic attacks, eating disorders, and stress-related illness.

6. Direct Hospital Payment

BMI Travel Assist can coordinate direct payment to hospitals on your behalf. Here is how it works:

  • Contact the 24/7 assistance line as soon as possible. The number is printed on your policy voucher.
  • The assistance center will coordinate with the hospital to arrange direct billing whenever possible.
  • Pre-authorization is required for non-emergency admissions. You must contact the assistance center before receiving treatment for planned or non-urgent care.
  • Direct hospital payment is not guaranteed for all facilities. Some hospitals or clinics may not accept direct billing arrangements, in which case you will need to pay out of pocket and submit a claim for reimbursement.
  • Any medical expense not previously authorized by the assistance center may be denied.

7. Claims Filing

If you incur covered expenses, you must file a claim promptly:

  • Deadline: All claims must be filed within 30 calendar days of your voucher expiry date. After this deadline, no claims will be accepted regardless of the reason for delay.
  • Required documentation: Claims must include original medical reports and discharge summaries, itemized receipts and invoices for all expenses, proof of payment, and any supporting documents relevant to the claim.
  • Theft or loss: For claims involving theft of baggage or personal belongings, a police report filed within 24 hours of the incident is required.
  • Incomplete claims may be delayed or denied. Ensure all documentation is complete before submission.

8. Cancellation Policy

Your plan can be cancelled under the following conditions:

  • Before departure: A full refund is available if the plan is cancelled before the coverage start (departure) date.
  • After coverage begins: No cancellation or refund is possible once the coverage period has started, regardless of whether any benefits have been used.

9. Waiting Period

If the plan is purchased while the beneficiary is already at the destination (outside their country of residence), waiting periods apply:

  • 5 days for all benefits except COVID-19.
  • 15 days for COVID-19 coverage.

Any medical event, illness, or injury that occurs during the waiting period is not covered during or after the waiting period, even if symptoms persist or worsen after the waiting period ends. No waiting period applies when the plan is purchased before departing from the country of residence.

10. COVID-19 Coverage

COVID-19 coverage is available with the following conditions:

  • Age limit: COVID-19 coverage is available up to age 75.
  • Vaccination requirement: The insured must provide proof of complete vaccination with a WHO-approved COVID-19 vaccine. Incomplete vaccination or vaccination with non-WHO-approved vaccines does not qualify.
  • If the beneficiary did not follow travel restrictions, prophylactic treatment, or vaccination recommendations related to COVID-19 or other endemic/pandemic diseases, coverage may be denied.

11. Excluded Countries

No coverage is available for travel to the following countries: Iraq, Ethiopia, Syria, Ukraine, Yemen, Sudan, Somalia, North Korea, Israel, Afghanistan, and Cuba.

Limited coverage (subject to sanctions and special licensing requirements): Venezuela, Russia, and Belarus.

This list is subject to change based on geopolitical conditions. Visit bmitravelassist.com for the most current list of excluded countries.

12. Dispute Resolution

Any disputes arising from or related to BMI Travel Assist protection plans shall be submitted to the jurisdiction of the courts of the State of Florida, USA. This applies to all claims, coverage disputes, and contractual disagreements between the covered traveler and the provider.

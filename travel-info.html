Understanding Travel Risks When Visiting the United States

The United States remains one of the most visited countries in the world, welcoming millions of international travelers every year. However, long-distance travel can involve unexpected disruptions including:

  • Airline delays
  • Severe weather events
  • Immigration processing delays
  • Lost luggage
  • Medical emergencies while abroad

Travel assistance programs help travelers manage these situations by providing global support services, travel coordination, and emergency assistance while away from home.

Recent Travel Disruptions Affecting U.S. Travelers

2023–2024

Major winter storms disrupted flights across the U.S. Northeast and Midwest. Summer travel demand reached record levels.

2025

Several major airports experienced operational delays due to weather and airline scheduling changes.

2026

International travel demand continues to grow, increasing congestion at major U.S. airports.

For travelers arriving from overseas, disruptions can lead to:

  • • Missed connecting flights
  • • Unexpected overnight hotel stays
  • • Delayed baggage
  • • Changes to travel itineraries
